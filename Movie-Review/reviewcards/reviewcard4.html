Bachelor Movie Review:
Darling (GV Prakash Kumar), the protagonist of Bachelor, is the kind of guy who doesn't care what others think of him. He is an aimless youngster from Coimbatore, working in an IT firm in Bangalore, staying with his friends, who also hail from his native. He is in that phase in which he is enjoying the freedom of living by himself, free from the restrictions that living with one's family brings. Like the roosters in the mansion which he shares with his friends, he struts around with hardly a care. The only two things on his mind seem to be getting a bottle to drink and snagging a girl to snog.

And when he meets Subbu (Divyabharathi), a friend of a friend who is working in his office and happens to be single, all he sees is a prospective girlfriend. He worms his way into sharing her flat, and starts stalking her. She seems wise to his ways, but one thing leads to another, and soon, they are in a live-in relationship. Their raging hormones ensure that everything feels hunky-dory (this is not a romantic film!), until... Subbu becomes pregnant. She wants to keep the baby, and he, obviously, doesn't! He, in fact, is insistent that she aborts the baby. Where does this cold splash of reality take them next?

With a tone that could best be described as Arjun Reddy-meets-Angamaly Diaries, Sathish Selvakumar's Bachelor is an often fascinating, indulgent peek into the life of a deeply flawed character. His Darling is a character new to our cinema.. a self-centred, carefree young man with hardly any redeeming qualities. The film doesn't judge him, but merely portrays him as a confused young man who is incapable of being empathetic to those around him (he doesn't worry even when his actions land his mother and sister in prison). In short, he is not a character who is likeable, and Sathish doesn't try to make him so. Though there is some glorification of the character, at least initially, the filmmaker balances it by turning him into a joke later. In a delicious irony, we get to see this man, who boldly admits to masturbating many times in a day, reduced to calling himself as impotent. And GV Prakash Kumar does a pretty good job in capturing the obnoxiousness of this character. ubbu, on the other hand, is a normal young woman, who seems wise to the ways of Darling (she even tells him that boys from small towns tend to romanticise any girl they come in contact with), but still can't help falling for him. Newcomer Divyabharathi is a pleasant surprise, and her expressions capture this character's short-lived joy and helplessness.

For most parts of the film, watching these contrasting characters get into a relationship and then struggle to get out of it makes for an engaging watch. It helps that the handheld shots that Sathish relies on (Theni Eswar is the cinematographer) to narrate this tale makes us feel like we are in the midst of these characters and being part of their lives. The director also peppers the scenes with humour, especially in the second half, when the film turns into a bizarre courtroom drama, to make it entertaining.

What brings down the film is the indulgent treatment. Sathish seems to be in love with this material and the characters that he lets them take over the narrative. Both the writing and the editing give the feeling of requiring tighter focus. The initial scenes involving the shenanigans of Darling and his friends seem to go and on and have a rambling quality to them. Similarly, in the second half, we get a funny but overlong episode involving a cameo by a filmmaker. The film also pushes the leads, especially Subbu, to the background once the court proceedings begin. It helps that the supporting cast (from Bagavathi Perumal to Arun Kumar, Dhanam and Munishkanth) is strong, but still, this shift in focus is jarring. In fact, we never see what the fallout between the couple means for them emotionally. Instead, we only get how it impacts the lives of those around him. This is why the ending feels less impactful despite being a progressive one.

காதல் திரைப்படமாக உருவாகும் பேச்சிலர் திரைப்படத்திற்கு ஒளிப்பதிவாளராக தேனி ஈஸ்வர் மற்றும் படத்தொகுப்பாளர் சான் லோகேஷ் இணைந்து பணியாற்றியுள்ளனர்.

ரிலீஸ் பேச்சிலர் திரைப்படத்தின் ஃபரஸ்ட் லுக் போஸ்டர் 2019 செப்டம்பர் மாதம் தொடக்கத்தில் வெளியாகி ரசிகர்கள் மத்தியில் பிரபலமாக விமர்சிக்கப்பட்டது.
